YTD Production Performance

YTD FFB achieved: 15,837.84 MT vs Budget 18,628.54 MT.
15% variance noted due to seasonal low cropping and productivity levels. (Pg. 52)

Key Observations & Issues

  • Low Crop Production: FFB production is 15% below the seasonal budget (16.57 MT/ha vs 19.49 MT/ha target). Achieving budget in the remaining months will require maximizing recovery. (PA Pg. 7, 52)
  • High Production Cost: Actual cost of production is RM 380.84/ton, 25% higher than budget, primarily due to the crop shortfall and higher upkeep/cultivation expenses. (PA Pg. 7, 53)
  • Vehicle Running Costs: Running costs for farm tractors and mini-tractors exceed budget due to high repair costs and low usage. (PA Pg. 8, 58)
  • Weed Management (Asystasia): While circles are generally clean, patches of Asystasia are growing vigorously in some areas (e.g., DB12-12A1) and must be eradicated during interrow spraying. (PA Pg. 5, 24)
  • Mechanization Opportunities: Manual cutter and carrier system is still used exclusively. Management is urged to introduce mechanized infield collection and composting to enhance productivity. (PA Pg. 8, 46)
  • Road Conditions: Surfacing of agricultural roads is still in progress (70% accessible). 30% remain impassable during rain, causing potential evacuation bottlenecks. (PA Pg. 40, 62)
  • Rat Infestation (Nov '25 Ref): Census from previous visit showed rat damage >5% in specific blocks (DG23, DH23, DK21). Management must ensure baiting followed previous recommendations. (AR Pg. 42-43)

Executive Summary:

The estate's performance is generally satisfactory. Crop production of 15,837 MT is 15% below the seasonal budget, and the cost of production (RM 380.84/t) is 25% above budget. Management is urged to focus on enhancing worker productivity and introducing mechanization for FFB infield collection, manuring, and composting to optimize costs. (PA Report, Pg. 8, 61, 63)

Area Statement:

As of December 2025, the total planted area is 958.78 ha. This comprises:

  • Mature: 955.80 ha (99.69% of planted area).
  • Immature: 2.98 ha (planted in 2023). This area is expected to be declared mature in April 2027.
  • Age Profile: 98.99% of palms are in the prime stage (13 to 17 years old).
(PA Report, Pg. 10, 12)

Labour Statement:

The current workforce is 427 workers, which is more than adequate compared to the requirement of 357:

  • Harvesters: 174 workers (Ratio 1:17 ha). Acceptable given no mechanization is yet implemented.
  • Spraying: 49 workers (Ratio 1:59 ha). Exceeds requirement of 28.
  • Manuring: 62 workers (Ratio 1:47 ha). Includes surplus of 16 workers.
  • General Workers: 82 workers including drivers, security, and workshop.
(PA Report, Pg. 15-16)

Staff Establishment:

The staff establishment is satisfactory with a total of 7 executives, including the Estate Manager (Bpk Joko Hidayat), 5 Assistant Managers, and 1 KTU. Each division is overseen by a Field Supervisor (4 in total). The ratio of 1 Assistant Manager to 578 hectares is considered adequate. (PA Report, Pg. 13-14)

General Charges:

Total General Charges are RM 1,468/ha (IDR 6,048,560), which is 9% below the budget:

  • Overspending: Noted in Staff Other Cost/THR (109%), General Office (70%), Computer Maintenance (146%), and Misc Expenses (549% due to travel).
  • Savings: Offset by lower Labour Overhead (51% below budget) and Staff Mess expenses.
(PA Report, Pg. 17-19, 64)

Upkeep and Cultivation:

Overall cost is RM 3,351/ha (30% above seasonal budget):

  • Weeding: Circle/Path spraying is 71% complete for the year. Selective spraying is 70% complete. Cost is slightly above budget (2%) at RM 99/ha.
  • Manuring: 99% of the annual program completed (1,398.58 tons applied). Actual cost RM 2,029/ha.
  • Composting: RM 686/ha (43% above budget) due to accelerated work progress.
  • Roads & Bridges: RM 343/ha (2% above budget). Surfacing of agricultural roads is ongoing with laterite soil.
(PA Report, Pg. 20-30, 33-35)

Harvesting and Collection:

Cost per tonne is RM 60.77 (IDR 250,429), which is 7% below budget:

  • Intervals: Maintained within 10-12 days for most areas (Good).
  • Productivity: Prime palm harvesters achieved 1.17 to 1.55 MT/md.
  • Mechanization: None currently used for infield collection; management urged to restart.
(PA Report, Pg. 46-51)

Crop Production:

YTD (8 Months) FFB Production is 15,837.84 MT, which is 15% below the budget of 18,628.54 MT.

  • Yield/Ha: 16.57 MT/ha vs 19.49 MT/ha target.
  • Extraction: CPO OER at 20.01% vs budget 21.50%.
(PA Report, Pg. 52)

Production Cost:

Total production cost (Ex-Estate) is RM 380.84/MT, 25% unfavorable compared to budget:

  • General Charges: RM 94.19/MT (+8% over budget).
  • Upkeep & Cultivation: RM 202.23/MT (+53% over budget).
  • Harvesting & Collection: RM 49.33/MT (-8% below budget).
(PA Report, Pg. 53-54)

Vehicles Running Cost:

Vehicle costs are generally higher than budgeted. Farm tractors for weeding/manuring are 13% above budget. Mini-tractors (Yanmar/Startrac) used for transport exceed budget due to high repair costs. Heavy machinery (Grader/Bomag) also shows high running costs. (PA Report, Pg. 57-59)

Capital Expenditure:

Actual cost to date is IDR 1.76 Billion (42% of annual budget). Primary expenditures include RM 446,354 for agricultural road lateriting and RM 13,112 for HDPE spiral pipes and culverts. (PA Report, Pg. 55-56)

Immature:

Field DG18-23A (2.98 ha) is in acceptable condition. Weeding has been carried out using Glyphosate. Immature harvesting began in August 2025. Full maturity declaration is set for 2027. (PA Report, Pg. 60)

Executive Summary:

Yield achieved 28.96 MT/Ha in FY 24/25. Key Issues: 27% of estate is flood-prone. Rat damage exceeds 5% threshold. Nutrient status: Low P, Marginal N. (AR Pg. 4, 7)

Nutrient Inputs/Progress of Manuring:

96% completion as of Oct '25. Lumpy fertilizer application observed in DG14. Avoid composting in flood-prone areas. (AR Pg. 11-17)

Agronomic Observation:

Harvesting intervals good. 5-8% overripe bunches in DG15/16. Drainage maintenance critical for 258 ha flood-prone peat. Rat baiting required Dec '25. (AR Pg. 18-43)

Yield Performance:

FY 24/25 reached record high of 28.96 MT/ha. Prime age palms (98.9%) provide high potential. (AR Pg. 44-46)

Agricultural Policy Cross-Reference

Mechanization (FFB Collection)

Policy Ref: MECH-FFB-01: Estates with flat terrain are required to implement mechanized infield collection (mini-crawlers) to improve evacuation efficiency and worker income.

Pest Control (Rats)

Policy Ref: P&D-Rat-01: A rat census must be conducted regularly. If fresh damage exceeds 5%, a baiting campaign must be initiated immediately.

Harvesting Intervals

Policy Ref: HARV-INT-02: Harvesting intervals must be maintained within 10-12 days to optimize ripeness and harvester productivity.
