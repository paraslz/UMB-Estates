Key Observations & Issues

  • Low Crop Production & Yield Decline: FFB production is 15% below the YTD budget. The full FY25/26 yield declined by 18% to 23.69 MT/ha, primarily driven by a 30% reduction in bunch numbers per palm (10.86/palm). (PA Pg. 52, AR Pg. 53)
  • High Production Cost: Actual ex-estate cost of production is RM 380.84/ton, which is 25% higher than budget, largely exacerbated by the crop shortfall and vehicle repair costs. (PA Pg. 53, 58)
  • Harvesting Quality & Losses: While the harvesting interval improved to 9 days, the incidence of overripe bunches remains high (5-9%). Crucially, uncollected bunches are frequently noted left in field drains. (AR Pg. 21, 25)
  • Flood-Prone Topography: Approximately 258.76 ha (26.86% of the estate) is low-lying and prone to prolonged flooding. This creates challenges in applying fertilizer and managing aggressive fern species. (AR Pg. 43, 55)
  • Mechanization Delays: The estate still relies entirely on a manual cutter and carrier system for infield collection. Introducing mechanization is critically urged to lower costs. (PA Pg. 46)
  • Pest Control Efficiency: Rat infestation is now minimal and well-controlled following the late 2025 campaign. To maintain efficiency, transitioning to a second-generation bait is strongly recommended. (AR Pg. 49-50)

Executive Summary:

The estate's performance is generally satisfactory. Crop production of 15,837 MT is 15% below the seasonal budget, and the cost of production (RM 380.84/t) is 25% above budget. Management is urged to focus on enhancing worker productivity and introducing mechanization for FFB infield collection, manuring, and composting to optimize costs. (PA Report, Pg. 8, 61, 63)

Area Statement:

As of December 2025, the total planted area is 958.78 ha. This comprises:

  • Mature: 955.80 ha (99.69% of planted area).
  • Immature: 2.98 ha (planted in 2023). This area is expected to be declared mature in April 2027.
  • Age Profile: 98.99% of palms are in the prime stage (13 to 17 years old).
(PA Report, Pg. 10, 12)

Labour Statement:

The current workforce is 427 workers, which is more than adequate compared to the requirement of 357:

  • Harvesters: 174 workers (Ratio 1:17 ha). Acceptable given no mechanization is yet implemented.
  • Spraying: 49 workers (Ratio 1:59 ha). Exceeds requirement of 28.
  • Manuring: 62 workers (Ratio 1:47 ha). Includes surplus of 16 workers.
  • General Workers: 82 workers including drivers, security, and workshop.
(PA Report, Pg. 15-16)

Staff Establishment:

The staff establishment is satisfactory with a total of 7 executives, including the Estate Manager (Bpk Joko Hidayat), 5 Assistant Managers, and 1 KTU. Each division is overseen by a Field Supervisor (4 in total). The ratio of 1 Assistant Manager to 578 hectares is considered adequate. (PA Report, Pg. 13-14)

General Charges:

Total General Charges are RM 1,468/ha (IDR 6,048,560), which is 9% below the budget:

  • Overspending: Noted in Staff Other Cost/THR (109%), General Office (70%), Computer Maintenance (146%), and Misc Expenses (549% due to travel).
  • Savings: Offset by lower Labour Overhead (51% below budget) and Staff Mess expenses.
(PA Report, Pg. 17-19, 64)

Upkeep and Cultivation:

Overall cost is RM 3,351/ha (30% above seasonal budget):

  • Weeding: Circle/Path spraying is 71% complete for the year. Selective spraying is 70% complete. Cost is slightly above budget (2%) at RM 99/ha.
  • Manuring: 99% of the annual program completed (1,398.58 tons applied). Actual cost RM 2,029/ha.
  • Composting: RM 686/ha (43% above budget) due to accelerated work progress.
  • Roads & Bridges: RM 343/ha (2% above budget). Surfacing of agricultural roads is ongoing with laterite soil.
(PA Report, Pg. 20-30, 33-35)

Harvesting and Collection:

  • Intervals: Maintained within 10-12 days for most areas (Good).

  • Mechanization: None currently used for infield collection; management urged to restart.
(PA Report, Pg. 46-51)

Vehicles Running Cost:

Vehicle costs are generally higher than budgeted. Farm tractors for weeding/manuring are 13% above budget. Mini-tractors (Yanmar/Startrac) used for transport exceed budget due to high repair costs. Heavy machinery (Grader/Bomag) also shows high running costs. (PA Report, Pg. 57-59)

Capital Expenditure:

Actual cost to date is IDR 1.76 Billion (42% of annual budget). Primary expenditures include RM 446,354 for agricultural road lateriting and RM 13,112 for HDPE spiral pipes and culverts. (PA Report, Pg. 55-56)

Immature:

Field DG18-23A (2.98 ha) is in acceptable condition. Weeding has been carried out using Glyphosate. Immature harvesting began in August 2025. Full maturity declaration is set for 2027. (PA Report, Pg. 60)

Executive Summary:

Overall agronomic conditions and upkeep practices have shown clear improvement, particularly regarding pruning and selective weed control. The FY2025/2026 FFB yield, however, declined by 18% to 23.69 MT/ha due to a lower bunch formation per palm. A notable success is the 100% completion of the fertilizer program and rat control stabilization. (AR Pg. 12, 31, 39, 50, 53)

Nutrient Inputs/Progress of Manuring:

The FY 2025/2026 fertilizer program was fully completed at 100% (applying 1,412.38 MT). Previous issues with lumpy fertilizer applications have been resolved. The estate is strongly advised to maintain proper frond stacking across undulating areas to limit runoff and improve nutrient uptake, moderating the strongly acidic soil pH (3.62-4.19). It is recommended to avoid dumping fresh compost in overly thick layers or in flood-prone areas to mitigate Ganoderma risks. (AR Pg. 12-19)

Agronomic Observation:

  • Harvesting: Average harvesting interval is stable at 9 days. Harvester loose fruit collection improved, but the rate of overripe bunches remains high (5% - 9%). Uncollected bunches left inside field drains were observed and require stricter supervision.
  • Pruning: Progressive pruning is 100% complete and highly satisfactory.
  • Ground Vegetation: Significantly improved. Bracken ferns and noxious weeds have reduced drastically. Selective interrow spraying using mist blowers is recommended instead of blanket spraying.
  • P&D (Rats): Rat damage is very minimal and well-controlled after successful baiting campaigns. Transitioning to second-generation rat baits is recommended.
  • Topography Challenge: 258.76 ha (26.86% of total planted area) are recognized as flood-prone low-lying lands, compounding the difficulty of operations and weed management.
(AR Pg. 21-50, 67)

Yield Performance:

The estate recorded a yield of 23.69 MT/ha in FY 2025/2026, representing an 18% decline from the peak yield of 28.96 MT/ha in FY 2024/2025. Analysis shows this decline stems entirely from a significant 30.33% reduction in bunch numbers (down to 10.86 bunches per palm), which overshadowed a 17.06% increase in average bunch weight (up to 15.30 kg). (AR Pg. 51-53)

Fertilizer Recommendation:

No amendments were made to the upcoming FY 2026/2027 fertilizer program. Management is reminded that the fertilizer program must be strictly followed without deviation. Strict supervision is needed to ensure fertilizers are never applied when soils are waterlogged. (AR Pg. 55-56)

Agricultural Policy Cross-Reference

Mechanization (FFB Collection)

Policy Ref: MECH-FFB-01: Estates with flat to undulating terrain are required to implement mechanized infield collection (mini-crawlers or similar) to improve evacuation efficiency and reduce reliance on manual cutter/carrier ratios.

Pest Control (Rats)

Policy Ref: P&D-Rat-01: A rat census must be conducted regularly. If fresh damage exceeds 5%, a baiting campaign using approved baits (e.g., second-generation anticoagulants) must be initiated and replaced at 7-day intervals.

Harvesting Intervals & Recovery

Policy Ref: HARV-INT-02: Harvesting intervals must be maintained within 10-12 days to minimize the percentage of overripe bunches. All harvested crop, including loose fruits and bunches in drains, must be evacuated to the platform on the same day.

Fertilizer Application

Policy Ref: FERT-APP-03: Fertilizers must be broadcast evenly along the inner edge of the palm weeded circle. Application must strictly be withheld in fields prone to flooding while waterlogged conditions persist.
